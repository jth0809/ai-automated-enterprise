Orbital propellant transfer demonstration

Engineers completed a full-scale cryogenic propellant transfer between two orbital tanks during the third integrated flight test, marking the first time such an operation has been demonstrated at this scale in the space environment.

The transfer moved 1,200 kilograms of propellant.

Telemetry reviewed after the test confirmed stable tank pressures throughout the operation, and engineers reported that boil-off losses remained within the pre-flight predictions used for mission planning purposes.

The agency said the demonstration retires one of the highest-priority technology risks on the roadmap toward crewed deep-space missions, which depend on refueling in orbit to carry sufficient propellant beyond low Earth orbit.

Independent observers noted that repeating the operation at an operational cadence remains the next milestone, and further flights are expected to demonstrate longer storage durations and automated couplings between vehicles.
